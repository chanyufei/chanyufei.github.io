Research Interests

My current research interest lies in the area of drug delivery and its applications to improve global healthcare. In particular, I have a strong interest in developing cost-effective drugs and novel drug delivery strategies, which will enhance the vaccination and treatment of major diseases including liver diseases, human immunodeficiency virus infection / acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (HIV/AIDS) etc. Innovative approaches such as pharmaceutical and immunological approaches will be undertaken to achieve this goal.

Current Research Project


Development of Intravaginal Ring-based Microbicides for HIV-1 Prophylaxis / in Dr. Emmanuel A. Ho's Group

In this project, we are designing, developing, characterizing, and implementing innovative intravaginal ring (IVR) microbicides for HIV-1 pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) or vaginal mucosal vaccination platform for other sexually transmitted diseases (STDs). As a multidispline project, techniques range from pharmaceutical scicences, cellular and molecular biology, bio-engineering, to immunology are widely utilized to optimize our system.

Previous Research Project


Role of L-FABP in NAFLD / in Dr. Frank J. Burczynski's Group

The goal of the this project is to explore the novel antioxidative function of liver-fatty acid binding protein (L-FABP) in non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD). In particular, we are focusing on utilizing a cell culture model to study elevated L-FABP expression as a potential protective intervention for conteracting the oxidative stress observed in liver cells challenged in high fat environment in vitro.

Brief Bio

Yufei Chen received his B.S. in Pharmacy Engineering from the Jilin University (China) in 2007. In 2009, he was accepted as a M.Sc. student under the supervision of Dr. Frank J. Burczynski, during which he obtained the vision in the field of liver disease and received cell/molecular biology training. After obtaining his M.Sc. degree from University of Manitoba in 2012, he joined Dr. Emmanual A. Ho's group, where he was promoted to the field of pharmaceutical sciences, aiming at the development of innovative intravaginal microbicides against HIV-1 infection. Currently, he is a senior member of the Laboratory of Drug Delivery and Biomaterials in the Faculty of Pharmacy, University of Manitoba.

Yufei has received a number of awards for his excellent academic and scientific research performance, including Manitoba Health Research Council - Faculty of Pharmacy Coordinated Studentship, MPhA / William G. Eamer Graduate Scholarship in Pharmacy, University of Manitoba Graduate Fellowship, and International Graduate Student Entrance Scholarship.
